# Project Overview

## Introduction

Ethio Language Box is a comprehensive national digital language platform designed to document, preserve, teach, and promote Ethiopian languages. The platform brings together learning materials, digital tools, audio pronunciation support, and community-based language contributions into one coordinated ecosystem.

Through books, a website, a mobile application, and audio-based learning resources, Ethio Language Box supports learners, educators, translators, editors, reviewers, administrators, and native speakers. It is more than a language learning tool: it is a cultural preservation initiative and a scalable model for multilingual countries seeking to protect and share their linguistic heritage.

## Vision

To become the leading digital ecosystem for preserving, learning, and promoting Ethiopian languages, while serving as a global model for inclusive multilingual documentation and education.

## Mission

To provide accessible, accurate, and community-supported language resources for all Ethiopian languages through integrated digital platforms, structured learning content, pronunciation tools, and collaborative language documentation workflows.

## Objectives

- Document and preserve Ethiopian languages in a structured digital format.
- Support learning across Ethiopia's 80+ languages through accessible educational materials.
- Provide reliable translation, editing, review, and approval workflows for language content.
- Enable native speakers and language experts to contribute to the quality and authenticity of content.
- Offer audio pronunciation resources that improve language learning and oral preservation.
- Make Ethiopian language resources available through web, mobile, print, and audio channels.
- Build a scalable framework that can be adapted by other multilingual communities and nations.

## Key Features

- **Comprehensive language coverage:** Designed to support all Ethiopian languages, including both widely spoken and less-documented languages.
- **Multi-platform access:** Provides language resources through books, a website, a mobile app, and audio pronunciation tools.
- **Structured content workflow:** Supports translation, editing, reviewing, audio recording, administration, and publication processes.
- **Community-centered contribution:** Involves translators, editors, reviewers, native speakers, and language specialists in content development.
- **Audio pronunciation support:** Helps learners hear and practice accurate pronunciation while preserving spoken language knowledge.
- **Digital learning resources:** Offers organized language learning content for self-study, classroom use, and community education.
- **Documentation and publishing tools:** Supports consistent content management, PDF generation, and delivery across digital and print formats.
- **Scalable preservation model:** Provides a practical framework that can be replicated in other multilingual environments.

## Stakeholders

- **Learners:** Individuals who want to study Ethiopian languages for personal, educational, cultural, or professional purposes.
- **Native speakers:** Community members who provide authentic language knowledge, pronunciation, and cultural context.
- **Translators:** Contributors responsible for preparing and translating language content.
- **Editors:** Language professionals who improve clarity, consistency, grammar, and accuracy.
- **Reviewers:** Subject matter experts who validate content quality before publication.
- **Audio recorders:** Contributors who record pronunciation and spoken language examples.
- **Administrators:** Platform managers who oversee users, workflows, content status, system configuration, and publication.
- **Educators and institutions:** Schools, universities, cultural organizations, and training centers that use the platform for teaching and preservation.
- **Ethiopian communities and diaspora:** Communities that benefit from continued access to Ethiopian languages and cultural knowledge.
- **Technology and project teams:** Developers, designers, documentation teams, and coordinators responsible for maintaining and improving the platform.

## Expected Outcomes

- A unified digital platform for Ethiopian language documentation, learning, and preservation.
- Improved access to language learning materials for Ethiopians and global learners.
- Better preservation of spoken and written language knowledge, especially for under-documented languages.
- Stronger collaboration between native speakers, translators, editors, reviewers, and administrators.
- Consistent language content supported by clear workflows and quality control.
- Increased visibility of Ethiopia's linguistic diversity through web, mobile, print, and audio resources.
- A reusable model for other multilingual nations and communities seeking to preserve and share their languages.

# System Architecture

## Overview

Ethio Language Box is designed as a centralized language content management and publishing system. The architecture connects contributors, quality assurance roles, a structured database, and multiple publishing outputs into one coordinated workflow.

The system supports the full language content lifecycle: authoring, translation, editing, review, audio recording, storage, approval, and publication. Approved content is then delivered through the Ethio Language Box website, mobile application, and PDF books.

## Core Components

### Authors

Authors create the original language learning content, source materials, lessons, word lists, phrases, cultural notes, and educational structures used by the platform.

Author responsibilities include:

- Preparing original learning materials.
- Organizing lessons and language content.
- Providing source text for translation.
- Supplying cultural and contextual information.
- Submitting content for translation and review.

### Database

The database is the central repository for all platform content and workflow data. It stores language records, translations, editorial updates, review status, audio references, user roles, and publishing-ready content.

The database supports:

- Language and dialect records.
- Lessons, words, phrases, and learning modules.
- Source text and translated content.
- Audio file metadata and pronunciation references.
- Contributor assignments and workflow status.
- Approved content for website, mobile app, and PDF generation.

### Translators

Translators convert approved source content into target Ethiopian languages. They ensure that translated content is accurate, culturally appropriate, and understandable for learners.

Translator responsibilities include:

- Translating words, phrases, lessons, and supporting materials.
- Preserving meaning, tone, and cultural context.
- Flagging unclear source content for clarification.
- Submitting completed translations for editing.

### Editors

Editors improve the quality, clarity, consistency, and readability of translated content. They ensure that content follows the platform's language and formatting standards before it moves to review.

Editor responsibilities include:

- Correcting grammar, spelling, and structure.
- Improving clarity and learner accessibility.
- Checking terminology consistency.
- Ensuring content follows platform guidelines.
- Sending edited content to reviewers.

### Reviewers

Reviewers validate the final quality of content before publication. They confirm that translations, edits, and language details are accurate and suitable for public release.

Reviewer responsibilities include:

- Verifying linguistic accuracy.
- Checking cultural appropriateness.
- Approving or returning content for correction.
- Confirming readiness for publication.
- Supporting quality control across languages.

### Audio Recorders

Audio recorders provide pronunciation and spoken language materials for the platform. Their recordings help learners hear accurate pronunciation and support oral language preservation.

Audio recorder responsibilities include:

- Recording words, phrases, lessons, and pronunciation examples.
- Following audio quality and naming standards.
- Submitting recordings for review and approval.
- Supporting the website and mobile app with pronunciation content.

## Content Lifecycle

1. **Content creation:** Authors prepare original learning materials and source content.
2. **Translation:** Translators convert the content into the required Ethiopian languages.
3. **Editing:** Editors refine translations for clarity, consistency, and quality.
4. **Review:** Reviewers validate content accuracy and approve it for publication.
5. **Audio recording:** Audio recorders create pronunciation files linked to approved language content.
6. **Database storage:** Approved text, audio metadata, workflow status, and publishing data are stored centrally.
7. **Publication:** Approved content is distributed to the website, mobile app, and PDF books.

## Detailed Component Explanation

### Authors

Authors are the starting point of the content workflow. They prepare the original educational material that will later be translated, edited, reviewed, recorded, stored, and published.

In Ethio Language Box, authors may create lessons, vocabulary lists, phrases, grammar notes, cultural explanations, examples, and other learning resources. Their work defines the structure and educational purpose of the content before it enters the translation process.

Authors help ensure that each language module has a clear learning objective and that the source material is complete enough for translators and editors to work accurately.

### Database

The database is the central system component that connects all contributors and publishing outputs. It stores the structured content and keeps track of each item as it moves through translation, editing, review, audio recording, approval, and publication.

The database contains text content, language metadata, translation records, editorial updates, review decisions, audio file references, contributor assignments, and publication status. It helps ensure that the website, mobile app, and PDF books are generated from the same approved source of truth.

By centralizing content, the database reduces duplication, improves consistency, and allows the platform to scale across many Ethiopian languages.

### Translators

Translators convert author-created source content into target Ethiopian languages. Their role is essential because they transform the original learning material into language-specific content that learners can use.

Translators focus on meaning, clarity, cultural accuracy, and natural language use. When content cannot be translated directly, they may adapt examples or wording so that the final result makes sense in the target language.

After translation, the content is submitted to editors for quality improvement and consistency checking.

### Editors

Editors review translated content and improve its quality before it reaches the final approval stage. They check spelling, grammar, structure, formatting, terminology, and learner readability.

Editors also help keep the platform consistent across languages by applying shared editorial standards. This makes content easier to publish and easier for learners to understand.

If an issue is found, editors may return content to translators for correction or clarification. Once the content meets the required standard, it moves to reviewers.

### Reviewers

Reviewers provide final validation before content is approved for publication. Their responsibility is to confirm that the content is accurate, culturally appropriate, and ready for public use.

Reviewers may be native speakers, language experts, educators, or subject matter specialists. They check whether the translation communicates the intended meaning and whether the learning material is reliable.

Approved content is marked as publication-ready and stored in the database for delivery through the website, mobile app, and PDF books.

### Audio Recorders

Audio recorders create pronunciation files for words, phrases, lessons, and other spoken language content. Their recordings help learners hear how language is spoken and support the preservation of oral language knowledge.

Audio recorders follow recording guidelines to maintain clear pronunciation, consistent audio quality, and correct file naming. Each audio file is linked to the related text content in the database.

Approved audio is used mainly by the website and mobile app, where learners can listen to pronunciation while studying.

### Website

The website is one of the main public outputs of the system. It presents approved language content online and allows learners to access lessons, vocabulary, translations, and pronunciation resources through a browser.

Because the website is connected to approved database content, updates can be published consistently after content passes the required workflow stages.

### Mobile App

The mobile app provides portable access to Ethio Language Box learning materials. It is especially useful for learners who want to study languages from phones or tablets.

The app can display approved text content and play approved audio recordings. It extends the reach of the platform beyond desktop users and supports flexible learning.

### PDF Books

PDF books are generated from approved content stored in the database. They provide a structured format for printing, offline learning, classroom use, distribution, and archiving.

PDF output helps make Ethio Language Box useful in settings where internet access is limited or where printed learning materials are preferred.

## Architectural Principles

- **Centralized content management:** All approved content is stored and managed through the database.
- **Role-based workflow:** Each contributor role has a clear responsibility in the content lifecycle.
- **Quality assurance:** Translation, editing, and review steps protect content accuracy.
- **Multi-platform publishing:** One approved content source can support website, mobile app, and PDF outputs.
- **Scalability:** The architecture can expand to support additional Ethiopian languages, contributors, and publishing formats.
- **Preservation-first design:** Text and audio records are managed as long-term cultural and educational resources.

# System Workflow

## Introduction

The Language Development Workflow explains how language content moves through Ethio Language Box from author submission to public release. The workflow supports accurate translation, quality assurance, audio pronunciation, website publishing, mobile app updates, and PDF book generation.

Each step must be completed carefully so that the final language content is reliable, searchable, teachable, and ready for long-term preservation.

## Workflow Summary

```text
Step 1: Author uploads statements
      |
      v
Step 2: Database stores statements
      |
      v
Step 3: Translator translates
      |
      v
Step 4: Editor edits
      |
      v
Step 5: Reviewer approves
      |
      v
Step 6: Audio pronunciation recorded
      |
      v
Step 7: Website updated
      |
      v
Step 8: Mobile app updated
      |
      v
Step 9: PDF generated
```

## Step 1: Author Uploads Statements

The workflow begins when the author uploads source statements, lessons, words, phrases, or other learning content into the system.

The author should ensure that:

- The content is clear and complete.
- The statement or lesson has a defined purpose.
- The source language text is accurate.
- Required topic or category information is included.
- The content is ready for translation.

## Step 2: Database Stores Statements

After upload, the database stores the statements and related metadata.

The database keeps:

- Source statements
- Topic and category information
- Language assignment
- Contributor assignment
- Workflow status
- Version history
- Publication readiness information

This step creates the central source record that translators, editors, reviewers, audio recorders, and administrators will use.

## Step 3: Translator Translates

The translator opens the assigned content and translates the source statements into the target language.

The translator must:

- Work in the correct target language field.
- Preserve the meaning of the source statement.
- Use correct grammar, spelling, and terminology.
- Avoid unnecessary additions or omissions.
- Complete translator self-quality assurance.
- Save the translated content in the system.

After translation, the content moves to the editor.

## Step 4: Editor Edits

The editor reviews the translated content for language quality, structure, consistency, and formatting.

The editor checks:

- Grammar and sentence structure
- Tense and verb forms
- Subject, person, number, and gender agreement
- Terminology consistency
- Pronoun and reference consistency
- Punctuation, spacing, and spelling
- Correct database entry and saved version

After editing, the editor approves the content for reviewer certification or returns it for correction.

## Step 5: Reviewer Approves

The reviewer performs final linguistic and contextual validation before publication.

The reviewer confirms:

- Translation accurately reflects the source meaning.
- Cultural context is preserved.
- Grammar follows the standardized rule framework.
- Terminology is consistent.
- Lesson alignment is maintained.
- No structural distortion is present.
- The content is ready for publication or audio validation.

Reviewer approval certifies the content for the next stage of the workflow.

## Step 6: Audio Pronunciation Recorded

After reviewer approval, audio pronunciation may be recorded for words, phrases, statements, or lessons.

The audio recorder must:

- Record from reviewer-certified text.
- Use clear articulation.
- Use natural native-speaker pronunciation.
- Maintain proper volume and clean sound quality.
- Avoid background noise and distortion.
- Name files correctly.
- Upload audio to the correct text entry.
- Test playback after upload.

Audio pronunciation supports the website and mobile app and helps preserve spoken language forms.

## Step 7: Website Updated

Approved text and validated audio are published or updated on the Ethio Language Box website.

Before website update, confirm:

- Content is approved.
- The correct language and topic are selected.
- Text displays properly.
- Audio playback works where applicable.
- No draft or unapproved content appears.
- Metadata is complete.

The website allows learners to access Ethiopian language content online.

## Step 8: Mobile App Updated

Approved content is prepared and released to the mobile application.

Before mobile app update, confirm:

- Approved text is available.
- Validated audio is attached to the correct text.
- Lesson order is correct.
- Language names display correctly.
- Ethiopian scripts render properly.
- Audio playback works.
- No duplicate or missing entries are present.

The mobile app makes Ethio Language Box content available to learners on phones and tablets.

## Step 9: PDF Generated

After content is approved, PDF books can be generated from the database.

Before PDF generation, confirm:

- The approved content version is selected.
- Language and topic information is correct.
- Lesson order is correct.
- Formatting is readable.
- Ethiopian scripts display correctly.
- No missing or duplicate content appears.

PDF books support printing, offline learning, classroom use, distribution, and archival preservation.

## Workflow Quality Gates

Each stage must pass a quality gate before moving forward:

- **Author upload:** Source content is complete and understandable.
- **Database storage:** Content is stored in the correct record with proper metadata.
- **Translation:** Target language content is complete and self-checked.
- **Editing:** Content is corrected and validated for reviewer stage.
- **Review:** Content is certified for publication or returned for correction.
- **Audio recording:** Pronunciation is clear, accurate, and linked to the correct text.

3. **PDF Books:** Automatically generates print-ready and offline educational materials for schools, researchers, and self-learners.

# Translator Manual

## Introduction

This manual guides translators through the Ethio Language Box translation workflow. It is based on the software training process prepared for translators and the **EILDP Quality Assurance (QA) Manual** prepared for the Ethiopian Indigenous Languages Development Project (EILDP), National Project Office, Ministry of Education.

The translator's role is to prepare accurate, clear, culturally appropriate, and technically correct translations for Ethiopian languages. Translator work supports the Ethio Language Box website, mobile application, PDF books, and audio pronunciation resources.

## Translator Responsibilities

Translators are responsible for:

- Translating source content into the assigned target language.
- Preserving the meaning of the English and Amharic reference content.
- Following approved grammar, terminology, spelling, and writing standards.
- Entering translations in the correct database field.
- Completing all required fields before submission.
- Performing self-quality assurance before sending content to editors.
- Preparing text that can later be edited, reviewed, recorded, and published.

## 5. Quality Standards

All translations must meet EILDP quality assurance standards before they can be edited, reviewed, approved, recorded, and published.

### Structural Accuracy

The translation must follow the approved grammatical structure of the target language.

Check that:

- Sentence structure follows the accepted grammar rules of the language.
- Correct tense is used, including present, past, continuous, and future forms where applicable.
- Subject, verb, person, number, and gender agreement are accurate.
- Affirmative, negative, and interrogative forms are correctly structured.
- Modal meanings such as ability, permission, obligation, or necessity are properly adapted.

### Tense and Verb Accuracy

Verb forms must be consistent and grammatically correct.

Check that:

- Present tense rules are correctly applied.
- Past tense forms are properly constructed.
- Continuous tense markers are used correctly.
- Future constructions are properly formed.
- Equivalent forms of "to be", "have", and "had" are aligned with the subject and meaning.
- Prefixes and suffixes follow the approved language structure.

### Terminology Consistency

Approved terminology must be used consistently across topics.

Check that:

- Approved glossary terms are used.
- No alternative word is used inconsistently.
- Technical terms are translated consistently.
- Dialects are not mixed without approval.
- Repeated terms are translated in the same way unless the context requires a different form.

### Pronoun and Reference Consistency

Pronouns and references must remain consistent throughout the text.

Check that:

- The same pronoun form is used consistently.
- There is no unnecessary switching between formal and informal forms.
- Gender references are consistent.
- Singular and plural forms are correct.
- Person reference remains clear to the learner.

### Cultural and Contextual Accuracy

Translations must preserve meaning while respecting the culture and normal usage of the target language community.

Check that:

- The translation respects cultural norms.
- Literal translation errors are avoided.
- Idiomatic expressions are adapted appropriately.
- Social politeness levels are maintained.
- Context-sensitive meaning is preserved.

### Technical and Database Accuracy

Translations must be entered correctly in the software.

Check that:

- The translation is entered in the correct database field.
- All required fields are completed.
- No duplicate entries are created.
- The correct version is saved.
- Changes are traceable through the system workflow.

### Formatting and Writing Standards

Text must be clean, readable, and ready for editing and review.

Check that:

- Sentence case is used correctly.
- First letters are capitalized where required by the writing system.
- Punctuation marks are used correctly.
- There are no extra spaces.
- There are no typographical errors.
- The text is readable and well structured.

### Language-Specific Structure

Each Ethiopian language may have unique grammatical or writing features. Translators must apply the correct structure for their assigned language.

Check that:

- Gender distinctions are applied correctly where required.
- Unique structural forms are properly used.
- Verb morphology matches the approved language structure.
- Special grammatical notes are identified when necessary.

### Audio Alignment Pre-Check

Translated text may later be used for audio pronunciation recording. Translators must prepare text that can be read clearly by audio recorders.

Check that:

- The pronunciation is readable from the written form.
- There are no structural errors that affect audio clarity.
- Words are clearly separable for recording.
- There is no unintended ambiguity.

## Translator Self-QA Checklist

Before submitting translated content to the editor, the translator must confirm:

### Step 3: Confirm the Correct Language

- The English and Amharic reference text is visible and understood.
- Translator self-QA has been completed.
- Editor validation has been completed and the edited version is saved in the system.
- Editorial remarks are available where applicable.

For example, if you are assigned to review Yem language content, make sure you are working in the Yem language field and not another language field.

## 2. Review Process

The review process requires the reviewer to examine the edited translation against the source material, validate linguistic and cultural accuracy, check cross-language consistency, and make a certification decision.

### Review Steps

1. Read the English and Amharic source content carefully before reviewing the target language translation.
2. Confirm that the target language translation accurately reflects the source meaning, with no important meaning omitted and no unrelated meaning added.
3. Validate linguistic accuracy: grammar aligned with the standardized rule framework, sentence structure, tense, verb endings, grammatical markers, subject, person, number, and gender agreement, pronoun consistency, and terminology consistency with the approved glossary.
4. Validate cultural context: cultural meaning is preserved, literal translation errors are avoided, idiomatic expressions are appropriately adapted, social politeness levels are suitable, and examples are culturally appropriate.
5. Check cross-language consistency: semantic equivalence is maintained, no structural distortion is present, and lesson alignment is consistent across related topics.
6. Perform audio-text synchronization pre-check: confirm that pronunciation can match the written text, verb endings and tense markers are clear, and the text can be read naturally by a native speaker.
7. Record the certification decision and document reviewer comments where required.

![Examine the edited content in the target language field against the source reference](../images/12.Translate_into_Traget_Language.jpg)

### Review Guidance

- Review for meaning accuracy, cultural appropriateness, and overall quality, not only surface-level corrections.
- Read editorial remarks from the editor before beginning the detailed review.
- Do not certify content that contains unresolved meaning issues, cultural problems, or grammar errors that affect learner understanding.
- If the translation requires substantial correction, return it to the appropriate workflow stage rather than approving it provisionally.
- If a language-specific rule is uncertain, document a comment and hold the content for clarification from a language expert or coordinator.
- All reviewer decisions and comments must be documented clearly so that corrections can be traced through the workflow.

- Cultural context is preserved.
- Lesson alignment is consistent.

# Audio Recorder Manual

## Introduction

This manual guides audio recorders through the Ethio Language Box audio pronunciation recording and validation workflow. It is aligned with the **EILDP Quality Assurance (QA) Manual** for the Ethiopian Indigenous Languages Development Project (EILDP), National Project Office, Ministry of Education.

Audio recordings support learners by helping them hear accurate pronunciation of words, phrases, lessons, and other language content. They also support the long-term preservation of Ethiopian languages by documenting spoken language forms in a clear and organized digital system.

## Role of the Audio Recorder in the QA Workflow

Ethio Language Box follows a staged quality assurance process:

```text
Translator Self-QA
      |
      v
Editor Validation
      |
      v
Reviewer Certification
      |
      v
Audio Validation
      |
      v
Publication
```

Audio recorders work at the **Audio Validation** stage. Recording should be based on reviewed and approved text so that the audio matches the final written content.

## Audio Recorder Responsibilities

Audio recorders are responsible for:

- Recording clear and accurate pronunciation for approved language content.
- Using natural pacing and authentic native-speaker pronunciation.
- Following language-specific pronunciation rules, including gender-based pronunciation where applicable.
- Recording in a quiet environment with proper volume and no background noise.
- Using the required file naming convention.
- Uploading audio files to the correct system location.
- Tagging metadata accurately.
- Synchronizing audio with the correct textual entry.
- Testing playback after upload.
- Re-recording or correcting audio when required.
- Documenting the recorder name, language, date, and approval decision where required.

## Recording Workflow

### 1. Confirm Approved Text

Before recording, confirm that the text has passed the required QA stages.

Check that:

- Translator self-QA has been completed.
- Editor validation has been completed.
- Reviewer certification has been completed.
- The text is the correct final version.
- The text is readable and ready for pronunciation recording.

Do not record from draft, unapproved, or unclear text unless instructed by the project coordinator.

### 2. Prepare the Recording Environment

Choose a quiet location before recording.

Before starting, check that:

- There is no background noise.
- The microphone is working properly.
- The recording device has enough battery or power.
- The recording volume is neither too low nor too high.
- The speaker can read the text clearly and naturally.

### 3. Review the Text Before Recording

Read the text silently before recording.

Confirm that:

- The pronunciation is clear from the written form.
- Words are clearly separable.
- Verb endings, tense markers, and grammatical forms are understood.
- There is no ambiguity that could cause incorrect pronunciation.
- Any gender-based or language-specific pronunciation rule is understood.

If the text is unclear, report it before recording.

### 4. Record the Audio

Record the approved text using clear articulation and natural pacing.

During recording:

- Pronounce each word clearly.
- Use natural native-speaker pronunciation.
- Keep a steady pace.
- Avoid rushing.
- Avoid long pauses unless required by the text.
- Do not add words that are not in the approved text.
- Do not omit words from the approved text.
- Re-record immediately if there is a mistake, noise, interruption, or unclear pronunciation.

### 5. Review the Recording

Listen to the recording before uploading.

Confirm that:

- Pronunciation is clear.
- The audio matches the written text.
- There is no background noise.
- The volume level is appropriate.
- There is no distortion.
- No word is missing or added.
- The pacing is natural.

### 6. Name the File Correctly

Use the required file naming convention provided by the project team.

A good file name should help identify:

- Language
- Topic or lesson
- Word, phrase, or content item
- Version or sequence number where applicable

Do not use unclear names such as `audio1`, `new_recording`, or `final_final`.

### 7. Upload and Tag the Audio

Upload the audio file to the correct system location.

After uploading, confirm that:

- The file is attached to the correct textual entry.
- Metadata is properly tagged.
- The language is correct.
- The topic or lesson is correct.
- The uploaded file is the correct version.

### 8. Test Playback

After upload, test the audio playback in the system.

Confirm that:

- The audio plays successfully.
- Playback matches the correct text.
- Sound quality is clear.
- The file opens without errors.
- The audio is ready for validation or publication.

## Quality Control Standards

### A. Pronunciation Standards

Audio recordings must meet pronunciation standards before approval.

Check that:

- Articulation is clear.
- Pacing is natural.
- Pronunciation reflects authentic native-speaker usage.
- Gender-based pronunciation is accurate where applicable.
- Words, phrases, and sentence endings are pronounced correctly.

### B. Technical Quality

Audio must be technically clean and usable for learners.

Check that:

- There is no background noise.
- Volume levels are proper.
- There is no distortion.
- The recording is not cut off at the beginning or end.
- The file follows the correct naming convention.

### C. System Integration

Audio must be correctly connected to the approved written content.

Check that:

- The audio file is uploaded correctly.
- Metadata is properly tagged.
- The audio is synchronized with the correct textual entry.
- Playback has been tested successfully.
- The correct file version is stored in the system.

### D. Audio-Text Synchronization

Audio must match the written text exactly.

Check that:

- Pronunciation matches the written text.
- Verb endings and tense markers match the written form.
- No words are added.
- No words are omitted.
- No pronunciation ambiguity remains.

## Validation and Approval Process

After recording and upload, each audio file must be validated before publication.

### Approval Decision Options

Use one of the following decisions:

- **Approved for publication:** The audio meets pronunciation, technical quality, and system integration standards.
- **Re-recording required:** The audio has pronunciation, noise, volume, distortion, synchronization, or file issues.
- **Correction completed and verified:** A previously identified issue has been corrected and the new version has been checked.

### Approved for Publication

Approve the audio only when:

- Pronunciation is clear and accurate.
- Native-speaker authenticity is maintained.
- Natural pacing is used.
- Technical quality is acceptable.
- The file name is correct.
- Metadata is properly tagged.
- Audio is synchronized with the correct text.
- Playback has been tested successfully.

### Re-Recording Required

Require re-recording when:

- Pronunciation is unclear or incorrect.
- Background noise is present.
- Volume is too low or too high.
- Distortion affects audio quality.
- The wrong text was recorded.
- Words were added, omitted, or changed.
- The file is attached to the wrong textual entry.
- Playback fails or the file is damaged.

### Correction Completed and Verified

Use this decision when:

- A previous issue has been corrected.
- The new recording has been checked.
- The file is uploaded to the correct entry.
- Metadata and playback have been verified.
- The audio now meets publication standards.

## Audio Recording Checklist

Before submitting audio for approval, confirm:

- I recorded from reviewer-certified text.
- I used clear articulation.
- I used natural pacing.
- I used authentic native-speaker pronunciation.
- I applied gender-based pronunciation rules where applicable.
- I recorded in a quiet environment.
- I checked that there is no background noise.
- I checked that volume levels are proper.
- I checked that there is no distortion.
- I used the correct file naming convention.
- I uploaded the audio file correctly.
- I tagged metadata properly.
- I synchronized the audio with the correct textual entry.
- I tested playback successfully.

Audio recorders must follow the national QA governance principles of the project:

- All linguistic content must pass Translator Self-QA, Editor Validation, Reviewer Certification, and Audio Validation before publication.
- Audio should be recorded from approved and current text only.

4. Refresh only after confirming whether content was saved.
5. Try a private or incognito window.
6. Report repeated errors to the administrator or technical team.

## Duplicate Content Issues

### Problem: Duplicate Entries Appear

Possible causes:

- User saved the same content more than once.
- Same topic was entered in multiple fields.
- Incorrect database assignment.

Recommended actions:

1. Identify the correct approved entry.
2. Mark duplicate entries for review.
3. Do not delete records unless authorized.
4. Confirm the correct version.
5. Update the workflow record.

## PDF Generation Issues

### Problem: PDF Has Missing or Incorrect Content

Possible causes:

- Content not approved.
- Wrong language or topic selected.
- Metadata missing.
- Database content not updated.
- Generated PDF used an old version.

Recommended actions:

1. Confirm content approval status.
2. Check selected language and topic.
3. Confirm metadata is complete.
4. Regenerate the PDF from the correct source.
5. Review the generated PDF before release.

## Audio Upload and Playback Issues

### Problem: Audio Does Not Upload or Play

Possible causes:

- Incorrect file format
- Large file size
- Poor internet connection
- Wrong system entry
- Damaged audio file
- Playback cache issue

Recommended actions:

1. Confirm the audio file is valid.
2. Check the file name and format.
3. Upload to the correct textual entry.
4. Test playback after upload.
5. Re-upload if the file is damaged.
6. Escalate if playback fails after a correct upload.

## Mobile App Display Issues

### Problem: Content Does Not Appear Correctly in the App

Possible causes:

- Content not approved for mobile release.
- App has not been updated.
- Metadata is missing.
- Wrong language or topic mapping.
- Script rendering issue.

Recommended actions:

1. Confirm content approval status.
2. Check language and topic mapping.
3. Confirm metadata is complete.
4. Test the app after refresh or update.
5. Report script rendering issues to the technical team.

## Workflow Status Issues

### Problem: Content Is Stuck in a Workflow Stage

Possible causes:

- Translator has not completed self-QA.
- Editor has not validated content.
- Reviewer has not certified content.
- Audio validation is pending.
- Status was not updated in the system.

Recommended actions:

1. Check the current workflow stage.
2. Identify the responsible user.
3. Review comments or correction requests.
4. Update the status after completed work.
5. Escalate delayed items to the project coordinator.

## Escalation Checklist

Escalate an issue when:

- User access cannot be restored.
- Content cannot be saved after repeated attempts.
- Approved content is missing from outputs.
- Audio or PDF generation repeatedly fails.
- Mobile app display problems affect many users.
- Data integrity or security is at risk.

# Frequently Asked Questions

## General Questions

### What is Ethio Language Box?

Ethio Language Box is a digital platform for documenting, preserving, learning, and promoting Ethiopian languages through website, mobile app, PDF books, and audio pronunciation resources.

### Who uses the system?

The system is used by administrators, authors, translators, editors, reviewers, audio recorders, educators, learners, and project teams.

### What are the main outputs of the platform?

The main outputs are:

- Website content
- Mobile app content
- PDF books
- Audio pronunciation resources

## User and Access Questions

### Who creates user accounts?

Administrators create and manage user accounts.

### What should I do if I cannot log in?

Check your username and password, then try a private or incognito browser window. If the problem continues, contact the administrator for account verification or password reset.

### Can one user have more than one role?

Yes, but multiple roles should be assigned only when approved by the project coordinator or administrator.

## Translation Questions

### When should translators save their work?

Translators should save after completing and reviewing each translation entry or topic. They should confirm that saved content remains visible before moving to another topic.

### What should translators do if the source text is unclear?

They should not guess. They should report the issue to the editor, reviewer, coordinator, or administrator.

### Why is translator self-QA required?

Self-QA helps ensure grammar, terminology, structure, cultural meaning, and formatting are checked before the content moves to editor validation.

## Editing and Review Questions

### What does the editor approve?

The editor approves content for reviewer certification. Editor approval is not final publication approval.

### What does the reviewer certify?

The reviewer certifies linguistic accuracy, cultural context, grammar alignment, terminology consistency, cross-language consistency, and publication readiness.

### Can content be published without reviewer certification?

No. Content should not be published without formal reviewer certification and final approval.

## Audio Questions

### When should audio be recorded?

Audio should be recorded only after the text has passed the required QA stages and is approved for recording.

### What makes an audio file acceptable?

Audio must have clear articulation, natural pacing, authentic pronunciation, proper volume, no background noise, correct file naming, correct metadata, and successful playback.

### What happens if audio has an error?

The audio should be re-recorded or corrected, then verified again before publication.

## PDF Questions

### When can a PDF book be generated?

A PDF should be generated from approved content that has completed the required QA workflow.

### What should be checked before releasing a PDF?

Check language, topic, lesson order, script display, formatting, missing content, duplicate content, file name, and final review status.

## Mobile App Questions

### When does content appear in the mobile app?

Content appears in the mobile app after it has been approved, prepared for mobile release, and included in the app update or content sync process.

### What should be checked in the mobile app?

Check text display, language name, lesson order, audio playback, metadata, duplicate entries, missing content, and script rendering.

## Troubleshooting Questions

### What should I do if content does not save?

Check internet connection, required fields, and duplicate entries. Try saving again and wait for confirmation. If the issue continues, contact the administrator.

### What should I do if content is assigned to the wrong language?

Do not continue editing or translating in the wrong field. Report the issue to the administrator so the assignment can be corrected.

### What should I do if the system shows old content?

Confirm that the latest version was saved and approved. Refresh the page or log in again. If the issue remains, report it to the administrator or technical team.

## QA Governance Questions

### What is the required QA order?

The required QA order is:

```text
Translator Self-QA -> Editor Validation -> Reviewer Certification -> Audio Validation -> Publication
```

### Why is version tracking important?

Version tracking helps confirm which content was translated, edited, reviewed, approved, corrected, and published.

### Who should approve final publication?

Final publication should follow project governance and should occur only after the required QA stages are complete.
